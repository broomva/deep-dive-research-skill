# Research Request Examples

This document provides example research requests that demonstrate how to use the Deep-Dive Research Orchestrator skill.

## Investment Due Diligence

### Request Template
```
Conduct due diligence research on [COMPANY/PROJECT] for investment evaluation.

Key research areas:
1. Financial performance and funding history
2. Market opportunity and growth trajectory
3. Product traction and user metrics
4. Competitive positioning and advantages
5. Team capability and track record
6. Key risks and mitigation strategies

Scope: comprehensive
Format: markdown
```

### Real Example
```
Conduct due diligence on a Series A investment opportunity in the meditation app space.

Focus on:
- Funding rounds and capital structure
- Revenue and unit economics
- User growth and retention metrics
- Competitive landscape and differentiation
- Team background and relevant experience
- Market opportunity for faith-based wellness apps

Generate professional reports suitable for investment committee review.
```

differentiate themselves from secular competitors.

Analyze:

## How to Make Your Own Request

1. **Start with the template** above for your research type
2. **Customize with your subject** - Replace [BRACKETS] with specifics
3. **Add your details** - What company, market, or product?
4. **Specify your focus** - What decisions will this inform?
5. **Set your scope** - How deep do you need to go?
6. **Submit for research** - The skill will generate reports

## Getting the Most from Research

### Tips for Better Results

1. **Be specific** - "meditation app for families" vs. "app"
2. **Define focus** - Investment? Competitive? Partnership?
3. **Set expectations** - Standard 2-hour research or 3+ hours?
4. **List key questions** - What do you really need to know?
5. **Provide context** - Why do you need this research?

### Using the Results

1. **Read executive summary** first (10 min)
2. **Deep-dive into relevant sections** (15-20 min)
3. **Extract key metrics** for your use case
4. **Review sources** for any you want to follow up on
5. **Share with stakeholders** for input and feedback
